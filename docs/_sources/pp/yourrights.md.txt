# 5. Your Rights and Choices

You have certain rights regarding your personal data. We are committed to helping you exercise these rights.

## 5.1. Overview of Your Rights

Depending on your location and applicable data protection laws, your rights may include:

- **Right to Access/Know**: The right to request and receive information about the categories and specific pieces of personal data we have collected about you, how it is used, and with whom it is shared.
- **Right to Correction/Rectification**: The right to request the correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data we hold about you.
- **Right to Deletion/Erasure**: The right to request the deletion of your personal data under certain conditions.
- **Right to Opt-Out**: The right to opt-out of certain data processing activities, such as the "sale" or "sharing" of personal information (if applicable) or receiving marketing communications.
- **Right to Data Portability**: The right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, and to transmit that data to another controller.

## 5.2. How to Request Data Deletion

To request that your data be deleted, or to exercise any of your other rights, please contact us using the contact information provided below.

**Process for Data Deletion**:

1. Submit Your Request: Please send an email to david.lutovac@datablooz.com with the subject line "Data Deletion Request."
2. Provide Necessary Information: In your email, clearly state your request (e.g., "I wish to delete my data associated with [your username/email address]"). To verify your identity and protect your data, you may need to provide specific information such as the email address associated with your App account, your username, or other relevant identifiers.
3. Verification: We will verify your identity to ensure the request is legitimate and comes from you.
4. Processing Your Request: Upon a valid and verified request, your personal data will be removed from our active systems. 

Please note that some data may persist in secure backup systems for a limited period due to legal obligations or technical recovery processes, but it will no longer be actively processed or accessible.
